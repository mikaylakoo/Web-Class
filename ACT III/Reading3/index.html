To me, interacting and reading a website is like having a bottomless bowl.

The famous behavioural economics testing of creating self-refilling soup bowls resulted people to consumpe 75% more of soup without noticing.

Similarly, our act of mindless scrolling leads us to be oblivious about the amount of content that we scroll through and never feeling like you are running out of things to read or look into.

This is the foundation that builds today’s social media like Instagram and Facebook - the news feed is continuously filled with relevant stories but the user continues to scroll to find “the end” which they will never reach.

The continuous installation of the endless scrolling mechanism also has led to the notion of

“FORO” - fear of running out among people who have grown to become addicted to mindless scrolling.
